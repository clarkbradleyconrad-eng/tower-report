Tower Analysis — 2027 Class · June 3, 2026
Texas enters the summer visit season with eleven commitments in the 2027 class and a trajectory that projects into a top-10 national finish if the staff closes on its remaining priority targets. The class is built around a blue-chip anchor at wide receiver — Easton Royal checks in above 97 on On3's composite — and a defensive front that gains real depth with Cameron Hall and Derwin Fields already signed on. The offensive line remains the most critical open position: Jackson Cook gives Texas a credentialed OL early, but the staff needs at least one more blue-chip prospect before early signing period to fully close that gap. At quarterback, the 2027 need is genuine and the staff knows it — a name to watch closely will emerge this summer. With official visit season opening September 1 and the summer camp circuit underway, the next six weeks are the most important stretch of this cycle.

Raleek Brown
From: Oklahoma State · 1,300 rush yds in 2025
The most experienced and explosive back in the Big 12, now in burnt orange. Brown brings home-run ability and is exceptional in the open field and as a pass-catcher. Immediate starter who gives Sarkisian a true featured back option.
Projection: RB1 starter · 180+ carries · 1,100+ yards

CB · Jr.
Derek Williams Jr.
From: Penn State · 2025 Big Ten All-Conference
Texas needed a true CB1 after coverage losses to graduation. Williams brings elite man-coverage skills and the physicality the SEC demands. With Colin Simmons as the pass-rush anchor, this defense has a legitimate ceiling.
