The Buckeyes should probably hear this one.

Texas defensive end Colin Simmons has made his goals for 2026 crystal clear: "National championship, dog, the national championship. All the individual rewards will come with it, but I just want to win. Win, win, win."

But before any trophy, there's a score to settle. On September 12, the Longhorns get their rematch against Ohio State — and Simmons has had that date circled ever since the Buckeyes beat Texas 14–7 to open the 2025 season. His message to Columbus? Direct. Unbothered. Cold.

"Second game of the season, all that nervousness, all that jittery is out the door. We're coming. There's business to be taken care of."
Colin Simmons — via Eleven Warriors
The Backstory

When Ohio State and Texas opened the 2025 season in Columbus, Ryan Day's squad controlled most of the game. The Longhorns couldn't get their offense going, and Simmons had a quiet day — just two tackles, zero sacks. For a guy who came in promising to wreck the Buckeyes' quarterback, it was a humbling afternoon.

But what happened next is what makes this 2026 rematch must-watch television.

Simmons bounced back from that opener and finished 2025 leading the entire SEC with 12 sacks, adding 15.5 tackles for loss, 43 total tackles, 15 QB hurries, 3 forced fumbles, and 2 pass breakups. He turned one of his worst individual games into the fuel that powered an SEC-best season. That's the kind of player he is — the kind that remembers, recalibrates, and returns.

And now he's hungry for the one thing that got away.

The Stakes

This isn't just another non-conference game. Ohio State won convincingly in Columbus a year ago — the Buckeyes felt in control the majority of the way. Now the rematch comes to Austin, to DKR, in front of 100,000+ fans in burnt orange. The roles are flipped, and so is the pressure.

Simmons also added a jab at Ohio State's quarterback situation, saying he's "just glad that they chose a quarterback that I'm ready to sack" — the same energy he's carried since before last year's game. Except this time, the résumé backs it up. He's not predicting. He's promising.

For Texas, beating Ohio State has become something bigger than a rivalry win. It's become a measuring stick. A statement. Every time the Longhorns have fallen short of a national title conversation, the Buckeyes have had a hand in it — directly or indirectly. Winning this game in September doesn't guarantee a championship. But losing it makes the climb a whole lot steeper, and everyone inside that program knows it.

The crowd will be primed. The preparation will be different. And the man they'll need the most has been waiting since November.

Bottom Line
Colin Simmons is one of the best edge rushers in college football. He led the SEC in sacks. He's got unfinished business. And he's telling you straight up — he's coming.

Ohio State should take that seriously.
