The Keys to the Season

1. The Arch Manning development curve. If Manning plays at the level his spring suggested — quick decisions, clean deep ball, pocket presence — Texas's offense is among the three best in the SEC. If there are growing pains early, the Week 2 Ohio State matchup becomes a genuine risk before the offense finds its rhythm.

2. The offensive line. We keep coming back here because it keeps being the honest truth. The Longhorns have a skill position group that can score on anyone. Whether they get time to operate depends entirely on five men whose SEC experience is limited. If the OL plays even competently, this offense is special.

3. Colin Simmons vs. everyone. When Simmons is wrecking games — sacking quarterbacks, disrupting screens, demanding double teams — the entire defense plays better. His presence makes Anthony Hill's life easier, it keeps safeties in the box, and it takes pressure off a secondary that needs Simmons to set the tone.

4. Winning the road games. Tennessee in Week 4 and LSU in Week 10 are as hard as any road games in college football. Texas went 2-2 on the road in the SEC last year. If they can steal one of those two, the path becomes much cleaner.

5. A&M on Thanksgiving. Whatever happens before November 27, this game will decide the CFP conversation. It always does now. Texas needs to arrive at Kyle Field with momentum. Sarkisian needs to have the team playing its best football of the year in November.
